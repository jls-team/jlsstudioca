Beginning

Before starting the project, I helped the team to create the proposal deck for the investors to pitch ideas for getting the Intellectual Property rights (IP). It was very exciting news for our team and the company when we finally got the IP! As a UI/UX designer, I have downloaded competitor’s games and started to play to understand the space for the game we are planning to make. Then create decks for each of the games:

  • Wrote down the pros and cons about games
  • Described game flow
  • Highlighted trendy UI elements with screenshots

I researched a lot of different datasets to understand things like - common phone screen sizes people use and the smallest ratio of the screen before creating a wireframe.

Working with teams

I always learn many things while I am working with the other teams

  • A lot of meetings with a game director, game designers, and writers to discuss the direction of the game.
  • Create a UI/UX visual target and direction deck to have better communication with the art team.
  • Meetings with the engineering team to find restrictions and problems that they are migrating while prototyping in Unity.

Design Process

1. Research
  • Meeting with game designers
  • Research IP/ understanding IP (study art style)
  • Research similar/ competitor's game
  • Read GDD (confluence guide that game design team made)
2. Low fidelity mock
  • UI/UX guidelines/ rules/ pillars
  • Game Screen Flow Chart (a master chart that showed how every screen in the game was linked together)/ basic layout/ functionality flow
  • Create Design Documentation for engineers
  • Feedback from game designers
3. Add graphic and UI
  • Make art direction
  • Come up with some samples
  • Create art skinning for each screen
  • Request arts you need from the art team
  • Communicate with game designers
  • Adding detail: font type/ style, Spacing, graphic
4. High fidelity
  • Working with the art team to finish up the mock
  • Communicating with the engineering team to transfer to unity
